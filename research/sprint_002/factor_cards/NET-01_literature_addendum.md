# Literature Addendum — NET-01 (Network and Protocol Activity)

**Card:** NET-01 (Sprint 001, Phase 2; priority raised)
**Sprint:** 002 refresh
**Status:** retained Phase 2; research priority raised

## What Sprint 1 already specified

Network/protocol activity normalized by scale as a Phase 2 candidate; requires methodology,
revision, mapping, and availability-time audit for every field before advancement.

## What the new literature changes

- LIT-033 (Li et al. 2026, PBFJ): tree-based ML shows market-to-realized-value, new addresses,
  and active addresses are the most influential crypto-return predictors.
- LIT-036 (Mohammad et al. 2026, Front. Blockchain): on-chain fundamentals (TVL, unlocks,
  burns, governance concentration) relate to ETH value; unlocks negative (β = −0.22).

## What remains unchanged

Phase 2 placement and the audit prerequisite are retained. NET-01 does not advance until
definitions, provider revisions, chain mappings, spam/entity effects, and availability
timestamps are auditable.

## Additional diagnostics (new)

Candidate concepts to preregister: active addresses / market cap, new-address growth,
transaction activity, protocol revenue/fees, MVRV-like measures, network growth,
token-function classifications. Test spam/entity effects and revision jumps explicitly.

## New data requirements

Point-in-time on-chain publication times and revisions (DF-07); chain mappings and
token-function taxonomies; provider-revision reconciliation.

## Why it remains untested (in this project)

Literature refresh only. NET-01 / H-007 stays `DEFERRED` until the on-chain data foundation
passes the audit gate.
