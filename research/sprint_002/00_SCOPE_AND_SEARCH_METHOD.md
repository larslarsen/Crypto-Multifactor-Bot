# 00 — Scope and Search Method

**Sprint:** 002 (literature refresh)
**Research cutoff:** 2026-07-18
**Prepared:** 2026-07-18

## Objective

Refresh the crypto multifactor evidence base with primary academic work published or
posted in 2025–2026, and convert the findings into explicit research-design decisions for
the existing factor families plus the new `DIL-01` token-dilution candidate. This sprint
records no empirical results from our platform and makes no validation claims.

## Databases and search engines used

- Publisher platforms: Wiley Online Library, Cambridge Core, Springer Nature, ScienceDirect
  (Elsevier), INFORMS PubsOnLine, Frontiers, MDPI.
- Working-paper / repository servers: NBER, SSRN, arXiv.
- Search engines: Google Scholar (forward/backward citation tracing), Semantic Scholar,
  and general web search for locating primary pages.
- Institutional references: BIS working papers (reached via publisher pages).

No German-localized search results were used. Where a source itself is in a non-English
language it was excluded unless directly and uniquely relevant; all 14 included sources are
English-language.

## Search strings (representative)

- `"cryptocurrency" momentum 2025 cross-sectional time-series`
- `crypto momentum crash volatility managed 2025`
- `"CTREND" cryptocurrency trend factor`
- `crypto carry funding basis staking 2025`
- `token dilution unlock cryptocurrency returns 2025 2026`
- `crypto factor model parsimonious cross-section 2025`
- `machine learning cryptocurrency return prediction illiquid 2026`
- `crypto factor zoo 2026`
- `cryptocurrency funding rate market microstructure 2026`
- `Ethereum tokenomics token value unlocks 2026`

Strings were adapted per database; no result was included solely on the basis of a high
Sharpe or prediction score.

## Date coverage

Primary search window: 2025-01-01 to 2026-07-18. Forward citations of Sprint 001 anchors
(Liu/Tsyvinski/Wu 2022; Cong/He/Tang 2025; Karassavidis 2025) were traced into 2026.

## Inclusion and exclusion criteria

Included:

- peer-reviewed crypto evidence (Tier A) or methodology (Tier B);
- crypto working papers with a primary source page and a stated sample/method (Tier C);
- surveys and factor-zoo compression studies directly relevant to our factor set.

Excluded:

- practitioner blog posts, news, and vendor marketing unless only used as a lead (never as
  supporting evidence for a promotion decision);
- pre-2025 work already in Sprint 001, except where cited as an anchor;
- papers we could not verify against a primary page;
- studies reporting a high Sharpe or accuracy as their sole contribution without a
  disclosed method/sample.

## Peer-reviewed versus working-paper classification

- **Tier A** — peer-reviewed, directly relevant crypto evidence.
- **Tier B** — peer-reviewed general methodology or survey, or an arXiv methodology paper
  with a primary identifier.
- **Tier C** — crypto working papers (SSRN/NBER working) with a primary page.
- **Tier D** — practitioner or exploratory material (used only as leads; none cited as
  supporting evidence here).

Practitioner sources may identify leads but must not support promotion decisions. The
seven-factor `Q-7` working paper (LIT-032) is Tier C and carries a declared commercial
conflict; it is recorded as a lead and a challenger, not as established evidence.

## Duplicate-paper handling

Each work was deduplicated by DOI / arXiv ID / SSRN ID. Where a working paper later
appeared in a journal (e.g. Scharnowski & Jahanshahloo liquid-staking work, posted 2024,
published Journal of Futures Markets 2025), the published version is the canonical ledger
entry and the posting date is recorded as the manuscript date.

## How publication date and version date were verified

For every source the primary page was opened and the following were recorded from the
publisher/repository metadata: authors, title, venue, publication or current-version year,
DOI/stable URL, and (where shown) received/accepted/published dates and version history.
arXiv entries record submission and last-revised dates from the submission-history block.
SSRN entries record posted and last-revised dates from the abstract page.

## Limitations of the search

- Access was via public primary pages; full texts behind paywalls were not downloaded.
  Bibliographic and abstract metadata from the primary page were sufficient for ledger
  verification.
- Search is not exhaustive; it targets the themes in the task brief. Relevant 2025–2026
  work outside those themes may exist.
- Citation counts and "verified" status reflect the primary page at the research cutoff and
  may change.
- Working papers (Tier C) are unrefereed and may be revised or not published.
