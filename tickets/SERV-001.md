## Objective

Bridge the gap between research and serving. Prove that the factors we developed in research (baselines, composites, ML) compute identically in an incremental (daily) context as they do in the historical batch context. This eliminates "research vs production" skew before we promote any artifacts to paper trading.

## Required Contract

- **Parity Harness**: A test harness that loops over `decision_time` chronologically.
- For each `decision_time`, it simulates a production invocation (only seeing data up to `decision_time`).
- It compares the incrementally computed `FactorFrame` against the batch-computed `FactorFrame` generated by the research substrate.
- **Assertion**: Values, scores, and included instruments must match exactly (to floating point precision limits, e.g., `math.isclose` or exact equality where applicable).
- Must test at least one of each factor type: a Baseline factor, a Composite factor, and an ML factor.

## Deliverables

- `tests/serving/test_parity.py` (or similar location)
- Minimal serving-wrapper if necessary to simulate the daily invocation.

## Out of Scope

- Real-time / live network data fetching (use the `_FakeAsOf` or `CatalogAsOfStore` with synthetic data).
- The actual paper promotion registry (that is step #23).
- State persistence for serving (keep the parity test in-memory/ephemeral for now).
